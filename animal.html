Mammals

Mammals are warm-blooded animals with fur or hair. They give birth to live young and nurse them with milk.

  • Lion - Known as the king of the jungle.
  • Elephant - The largest land animal.
  • Whale - The biggest mammal in the ocean.

Birds

Birds are feathered, winged creatures that lay eggs. They are found in nearly every environment on Earth.

  • Eagle - Known for its sharp eyesight.
  • Penguin - A flightless bird that swims.
  • Owl - A nocturnal bird with a distinctive hoot.

Reptiles

Reptiles are cold-blooded animals with scaly skin. They lay eggs and are often found in warmer climates.

  • Crocodile - A powerful predator with strong jaws.
  • Snake - Known for its slithering movement.
  • Lizard - Comes in various sizes and types.

Amphibians

Amphibians can live both in water and on land. They are often found in moist environments.

  • Frog - Known for its jumping ability and croak.
  • Salamander - Resembles a lizard, but is an amphibian.
  • Newt - Similar to a salamander with moist skin.
